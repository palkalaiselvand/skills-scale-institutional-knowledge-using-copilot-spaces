# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Technical Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
The Technical Lead provides architectural direction and technical decision-making for the project. They ensure the team adopts sound engineering practices and that solutions are scalable, maintainable, and secure.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the technical architecture and design standards
- Review and approve significant technical decisions and trade-offs
- Identify and mitigate technical risks early in the project lifecycle
- Mentor developers and support technical skill growth across the team
- Collaborate with the PM and PdM on feasibility assessments and effort estimates

### Goals
- Maintain long-term code quality and system health
- Reduce technical debt and architectural drift
- Enable the team to deliver confidently with well-understood constraints

### Typical Communication
- Architecture decision records (ADRs) and design docs
- Code and PR reviews for high-impact changes
- Weekly technical syncs with PM and PdM

---

## QA / Testing Lead

### Role Summary
The QA/Testing Lead owns the quality strategy for the project. They design test plans, coordinate testing activities, and ensure that acceptance criteria are met before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the test strategy and quality standards
- Write and maintain automated test suites (unit, integration, end-to-end)
- Coordinate manual QA activities for feature acceptance
- Track and triage defects, escalating critical issues to the PM
- Validate release readiness before each deployment

### Goals
- Achieve high test coverage and confidence in releases
- Minimize production defects and regressions
- Shift quality left by embedding testing early in the development cycle

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and QA reports shared with PM and PdM
- Defect tracking in the project board
- Pre-release sign-off and release notes review

---

## Security Engineer

### Role Summary
The Security Engineer ensures that the project meets security standards and compliance requirements. They perform security reviews, run scans, and provide guidance on secure design and development practices.

### Responsibilities
- Review architecture and code for security vulnerabilities
- Integrate security scanning into the CI/CD pipeline
- Respond to security incidents and coordinate remediation
- Provide guidance on authentication, authorization, and data handling best practices
- Maintain and communicate the security incident runbook

### Goals
- Prevent security vulnerabilities from reaching production
- Embed a security-first mindset across the development team
- Ensure compliance with organizational and regulatory requirements

### Typical Communication
- Security review findings shared with Technical Lead and PM
- Participation in planning sessions to assess security implications
- Security incident notifications to on-call and stakeholders

---

## UX / Design Lead

### Role Summary
The UX/Design Lead advocates for the end user throughout the project lifecycle. They design intuitive user experiences, validate designs with real users, and collaborate with developers to implement them accurately.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and synthesize insights into design requirements
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and final design specifications
- Define and maintain design system components and standards
- Collaborate with developers to ensure accurate implementation of designs
- Validate usability with stakeholders and end users before release

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce design-to-development friction through clear specifications
- Incorporate user feedback iteratively to improve product quality

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with developers and PdM
- Usability test findings shared with the team
- Design specs and prototypes linked in backlog items

---

## Scrum Master / Delivery Facilitator

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master facilitates agile ceremonies and removes impediments that block the delivery team. They coach the team on agile principles and help maintain a healthy, sustainable delivery pace.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers that impede team progress
- Track team velocity and flag capacity concerns to the PM
- Coach team members on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Shield the team from unplanned interruptions and scope creep

### Goals
- Maintain a predictable, sustainable delivery cadence
- Foster a culture of collaboration, transparency, and continuous improvement
- Reduce friction in ceremonies and ensure action items are followed up

### Typical Communication
- Sprint ceremony facilitation and notes
- Impediment log shared with PM
- Retrospective action item tracking

## How these personas are used in the exercise
- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When creating issues or pull requests for process improvements, reference the relevant persona(s) to provide context on who is affected and who is accountable.
